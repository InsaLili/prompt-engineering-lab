text: - **Data Transmission**: The internet operates through a vast network of interconnected devices that communicate using standardized protocols, primarily the Internet Protocol (IP) and Transmission Control Protocol (TCP). Data is broken down into packets, which are sent across various routes and reassembled at their destination.

- **Infrastructure**: The physical backbone of the internet consists of servers, routers, switches, and cables (including fiber optics) that connect devices globally. Internet Service Providers (ISPs) facilitate access to this infrastructure, allowing users to connect to the internet via wired or wireless connections.

- **Domain Name System (DNS)**: The DNS translates human-readable domain names (like www.example.com) into IP addresses that computers use to identify each other on the network. This system enables users to easily navigate the internet without needing to remember numerical addresses.
